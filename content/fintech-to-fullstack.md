# From FinTech to Full-Stack Builder

**March 2026** · 4 min read

My career started in finance. Islamic finance, specifically — microfinance research, corporate compliance, payment processing. But somewhere along the way, I realized I was spending more time building tools than using them.

## The Finance Years

At DOTS ERP, I built FinTech applications that processed payments 30% faster. At Kalite Global, I automated finance workflows across three countries. The work was meaningful, but I kept gravitating toward the engineering side.

## The Pivot

When I joined Yuze Digital, the role was "Software Engineer" — not "Finance Engineer." I was building bank onboarding APIs in .NET, architecting automation pipelines, leading QA for mobile apps. The finance domain knowledge was still valuable, but the craft was pure engineering.

## What Finance Taught Me About Engineering

1. **Precision matters** — In finance, a rounding error can cascade. Same in distributed systems
2. **Compliance is architecture** — KYC/AML rules are just business logic with legal consequences
3. **Automation is leverage** — Every manual process I automated freed someone to do higher-value work
4. **Data integrity is non-negotiable** — Financial data can't be "eventually consistent"

## Building in Public

Now I ship projects across the full stack. tarbiya.ai for AI-powered education. NoorBot serving 12k+ users. QueueFlow running in 40+ clinic branches. Each one started as a side project and grew into something real.

The common thread? Every project solves a real problem for a real person. That's the finance mindset applied to engineering — build things that create measurable value.
